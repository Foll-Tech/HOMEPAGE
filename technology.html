Our Technology Platform

A detailed look at the science driving Follicea Technologies — from target identification to drug candidate design and preclinical development.

The Problem

Infertility Remains Largely Underserved

Infertility affects approximately 1 in 6 people of reproductive age worldwide, yet the therapeutic landscape has seen limited pharmacological innovation in recent decades. Most treatments focus on symptomatic management — hormonal stimulation — rather than addressing the underlying molecular causes. Follicea Technologies was founded on the premise that precision, mechanism-targeted drugs can do better.

Our Approach

Mechanism-First Drug Development

We take a biology-forward approach: starting from deep mechanistic understanding of reproductive pathways, we identify targets where pharmacological intervention can restore function — rather than merely augmenting hormones.

1

Target Discovery & Validation

We use multi-omic datasets (transcriptomics, proteomics) from human reproductive tissue combined with model organism studies to identify proteins whose activity is causally linked to follicular development, oocyte maturation, or implantation. Validated targets undergo CRISPR functional studies to confirm essentiality and pharmacological tractability.

2

Hit Generation & Lead Identification

For each validated target, we run structure-based virtual screening and phenotypic assays against focused compound libraries. Confirmed hits enter iterative SAR (structure-activity relationship) campaigns to optimize potency, selectivity, and early ADMET properties. All hit-to-lead work is performed or supervised in-house, maintaining tight integration with biological readouts.

3

Lead Optimization

Lead compounds are optimized for oral bioavailability, metabolic stability, and selectivity over related targets. We employ computational ADMET prediction and early in vitro assays (CYP inhibition, plasma protein binding, permeability) to guide chemistry. Our goal is a drug-like molecule with a pharmacokinetic profile suitable for once-daily oral dosing.

4

Preclinical Development

Optimized leads undergo IND-enabling studies: rodent and non-rodent PK/PD, in vivo efficacy in relevant reproductive models, and a comprehensive safety/toxicology assessment. We work closely with CRO partners and our academic collaborators at Kyoto University to execute these studies rigorously and efficiently.

Platform

Core Technology Pillars

Two interconnected capabilities form the backbone of our drug discovery engine.

Reproductive Biology Intelligence

Our scientific team has deep domain expertise in folliculogenesis, oocyte biology, and ovarian signaling. We have built a proprietary knowledge graph integrating published literature, clinical datasets, and internal experimental data to rapidly identify high-confidence targets.

  • Multi-omic analysis of follicular cell populations
  • Human ovarian transcriptome datasets
  • Pathway mapping of FSH / LH / AMH signaling axes
  • Phenotypic assays in primary human granulosa cells

Small Molecule Drug Design

We focus exclusively on small molecule therapeutics — compounds that can be dosed orally, manufactured at scale, and distributed globally. Our chemistry team leverages structure-based design and computational methods to accelerate lead optimization.

  • Structure-based and fragment-based design
  • AI-assisted ADMET prediction & optimization
  • Medicinal chemistry focused on Lipinski compliance
  • CYP and hERG safety-by-design from early stages

Translational & Regulatory Strategy

Efficacy signals in vitro mean little without a credible path to the clinic. From the outset, we design our preclinical studies with regulatory submission in mind — selecting appropriate models, collecting biomarker data, and aligning with PMDA and FDA guidance for reproductive indications.

  • Early engagement with PMDA guidance documents
  • IND-enabling study design from day one
  • Reproductive toxicology planning integrated early
  • Biomarker strategy aligned with clinical endpoints

Academic & Clinical Collaborations

We actively collaborate with reproductive medicine departments and research institutes, providing access to patient tissue samples, clinical insights, and validation capabilities that de-risk our internal programs at every stage.

  • Kyoto University research collaboration (active)

Research Focus

Scientific Focus Areas

Our target selection is guided by unmet clinical need and biological tractability across these key areas of reproductive medicine.

Ovarian Biology

Folliculogenesis & Ovulation

Understanding and restoring the regulated process of follicle recruitment, development, and ovulation — disrupted in anovulatory conditions such as PCOS and hypothalamic amenorrhea.

Oocyte Quality

Oocyte Maturation & Competence

Addressing molecular defects in oocyte meiotic maturation and mitochondrial function that contribute to poor embryo quality — a major unmet need in older reproductive-age patients.

Endocrinology

Hormonal Axis Modulation

Small molecule modulators of the HPG axis that can fine-tune FSH and LH signaling with greater specificity and fewer side effects than current injectable gonadotropin therapies.

Male Infertility

Spermatogenesis Support

Although our lead program targets female infertility, we are actively evaluating male-factor indications where targeted small molecule intervention may restore spermatogenesis.

Implantation

Endometrial Receptivity

Investigating molecular determinants of endometrial window-of-implantation timing and receptivity to support embryo implantation success — relevant for both natural and ART cycles.

Precision Medicine

Patient Stratification

Developing biomarker strategies to identify patient sub-populations most likely to respond to our therapies, maximizing clinical benefit and supporting regulatory approval.
